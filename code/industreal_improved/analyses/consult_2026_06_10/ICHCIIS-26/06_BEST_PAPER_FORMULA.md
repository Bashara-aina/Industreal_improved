## What Wins Best Paper at a Small HCI Conference

Based on analysis of similar APSTE conferences and HCI venues, the best paper award at ICHCIIS-26 will likely go to the paper that best demonstrates:

1. **Completeness** — covers problem → solution → implementation → evaluation → ethics
2. **Practical impact** — solves a real problem for real people
3. **Interdisciplinary breadth** — connects multiple fields
4. **Clarity** — well-written, easy to follow
5. **Novelty within scope** — new approach to an HCI/information science problem

## The 5-Point Checklist for Best Paper

### 1. Lead with the human story
- First paragraph: worker problem, not technical architecture
- Use words like: "fair compensation," "worker empowerment," "transparency," "democratization"
- The $299 vs $10K+ comparison is your strongest narrative

### 2. Make the ethics section shine
- This is what sets you apart from every other technical paper
- IEEE 7005-2021 framework should be 20% of the paper
- The "surveillance vs empowerment" framing is memorable

### 3. Keep the technical parts accessible
- HCI reviewers are not CV experts
- Explain: "ConvNeXt-Tiny = a standard image understanding model" not "ConvNeXt-Tiny with 768ch C5"
- Use diagrams, not equations

### 4. Show you understand the limitations
- Honestly acknowledge: single dataset, single GPU, single seed
- This builds trust with reviewers

### 5. End with impact
- Last sentence: "democratizing access to AI-assisted manufacturing"
- Leave the reviewer feeling this matters

## Abstract Formula for Best Paper

1. **Hook** (1 sentence): The human problem
2. **Solution** (1 sentence): What you built
3. **Evidence** (2 sentences): Key numbers
4. **Ethics** (1 sentence): The governance framework
5. **Impact** (1 sentence): Why it matters

## Presentation Strategy (If Accepted)

If your paper is accepted and you present in Seoul:

1. **3 minutes**: The human problem + your system (with demo video if possible)
2. **2 minutes**: The ethics framework (this is your differentiator)
3. **1 minute**: Results + impact
4. **Q&A**: Prepare for questions on privacy, detection accuracy, blockchain necessity

**Visual aids:**
- Slide 1: Worker + camera + GPU ($299) + wallet icon — "Fair Compensation for Every Task"
- Slide 2: Architecture diagram (simplified)
- Slide 3: Cost comparison bar chart
- Slide 4: IEEE 7005 framework quadrants
- Slide 5: Impact — "From $50K to $299"
