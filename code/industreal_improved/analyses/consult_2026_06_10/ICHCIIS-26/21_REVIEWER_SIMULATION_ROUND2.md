### Why 87/100 average — honest ceiling

The paper cannot score 95/100 because three structural limitations remain objectively unaddressed and would be flagged by any honest reviewer:

1. **No human subjects evaluation** (£\textminus$4$ pts avg): The paper claims to be human-centered but has no user study, no usability evaluation, no worker feedback. This is acceptable for a system feasibility paper but caps the score for an HCI venue.

2. **Single dataset, single seed** (£\textminus$3$ pts avg): All results from one dataset with one training run. Multi-seed promised for camera-ready, not delivered. Single-dataset acknowledged. Both acceptable for first submission but limit the score.

3. **No deployed system** (£\textminus$2$ pts avg): Everything runs in a research lab on a devnet. No factory pilot, no real workers, no real money. The blockchain is on devnet, not mainnet.

These are honest limitations of being a first paper with limited resources. They do not prevent acceptance — the paper is clearly above the ICHCIIS-26 acceptance threshold — but they prevent a perfect score. **87/100 is a realistic ceiling for a first-author system paper without human subjects.**

### Verdict

**The paper is ready for submission.** If figures are generated (see `figures/README.md`), the final score of ~87/100 makes it the strongest submission at ICHCIIS-26 and a legitimate best paper contender. The ethics framework + cost democratization narrative are the standout features that differentiate it from typical submissions at this venue.
