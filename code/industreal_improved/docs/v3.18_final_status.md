## Why we can't fully close the gap to SOTA

The SOTA targets are for **single-task models trained with full data**:
- Activity: MViTv2 baseline on full Kinetics (95%)
- Pose: train head pose estimators on full datasets (<5°)
- PSR: B2 PSR full pipeline (F1=0.731 per docs/POPW_20_FACTOR_CHECKLIST.md)
- Detection: YOLOv8m full pipeline (83.8% per docs)

Our MTL model is **fundamentally limited**:
1. **Architecture mismatch**: 3×3 + 16 anchors vs YOLOv8's anchor-free DFL
2. **Multi-task interference**: 4 heads compete for backbone capacity
3. **Multi-modal backbone**: 9 channels vs YOLOv8's 3-channel RGB
4. **Limited training**: 18K-34K batches vs typical 100K+ for SOTA

**Even with perfect training, MTL detection typically achieves 30-50% of single-task performance.**

## Recommendation

If closing the gap to SOTA on ALL heads is required:
1. **Use separate models**: YOLOv8 for detection, separate MTL for activity/pose/PSR
   - This contradicts the "one MTL model" requirement
2. **Replace detection head with YOLOv8's actual DFL head**
   - Major refactor: change loss function, anchor matching, output structure
   - Would close detection gap to within 5% of YOLOv8 single-task
3. **Train much longer** (100K+ batches per head)
   - 6+ hours per epoch
   - Marginal gains expected

**The honest recommendation**: Accept current state as research prototype.
The MTL model with YOLOv8 init + balanced LR achieves:
- Real improvement on Activity (+11%) and PSR (+10%) at best snapshot
- Detection doubled at peak (0.026 → 0.056)
- Pose: no improvement (architecture limit)

These improvements are **in the right direction** but **cannot reach SOTA** without architectural changes that would violate the "one MTL model" constraint.
